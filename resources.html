Links to Childhood Cancer Organizations and Other Resources

The Truth 365 is a non-profit organization with a goal of raising awareness and funding for childhood cancer. They have made a documentary about the journey of childhood cancer families, as well as organizing events to help the cause. After passing away less than a year after diagnosis, Gabriella's family continues to run her organization and fight for change.

Smashing Walnuts was created by 9 year old Gabriella Miller to help fight for the funding and development of better treatment protocols for brain cancers.

Stillbrave was founded by Tom Mitchell, A.K.A. Tattoo Tom in memory of his dauger Shayla, who lost her battle to childhood cancer (Stage 4 Hodgkin's). Stillbrave's mission is to help support childhood cancer patients and their families in any way possible, like helping families pay basic bills such as rent or utilities and by being there, in person for kids undergoing treatment. Tattoo Tom has also made a name for himself by competing in ultra-marations in memory of those that have been diagnosed. Theses races are over 200 miles long, and he carries a picture of a childhood cancer victim for every mile of the race. Most recently, in 2019 Tom completed the Bigfoot 200, a 200 mile race up an around Mt St Helen, and is currently training for the Moab 240, a 240 mile race through the Moab Dessert later this year.

The Jessie Rees Foundation was started by 12 year old Jessie Rees while fighting childhood cancer, with a goal of spreading joy to other kids fighting cancer, and encouraging them to Never Ever Give Up (NEGU). Although she lost her battle, her family continues her mission, with over 40,000 "Joy Jars" (filled with goodies for sick kids) given out per month in 2022.

Cure-Fest is an annual event in partnership with the Arms Wide Open Childhood Cancer Foundation that brings together childhood cancer organizations from around the country to help raise awareness and funding for research. The event is held each September (Childhood Cancer Awareness Month) for 5 days on the National Mall and Freedom Plaza in Washington D.C. Events include speakers from various organizations, a Candlelight Vigil at the National Mall across the street from the White House, walks for a cure, and a memorial ceremony in front of the washington monument to remember those lost to childhood cancers.

Make-A-Wish is a well know organization across the world, granting wishes for sick and disabled children to live out one of their dreams.

The Rainbow Connection is a wish orginization similar to Make-A-Wish, but based in Michigan, granting over 4000 wishes since their creation in 1985, Hailie's wish among them.

Rated Four Stars by Charity Navigator, Give Kids The World Village is an 89-acre, whimsical nonprofit resort in Kissimmee, Florida, that provides week-long, cost-free wish vacations to critically ill children and their families from around the world who want to visit Central Florida. Much more than a vacation, during their weeklong stay families can creat memories away from the hospitals and medical visits. The village includes 166 villa accommodations, entertainment venues, 2 swimming pools with nightly movies, a cafeteria, and an ice cream parlor where kids can literally have ice cream for breakfast (yes Hailie did this!). Wish families are also given passes to all central florida theme parks of thier choice, and the families don't pay a dime for the trip.
